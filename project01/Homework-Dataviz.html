Chart 1 - relationship between carat and price

ggplot(diamonds %>% sample_n(500),
       mapping = aes(carat, price, col = cut))+
  geom_point(size=5, alpha=0.5)

We found a positive relationship between carat and price. If the carat increases, the price will also increase.

Chart 2 - relationship between carat and price by FACET

set.seed(42)
ggplot(diamonds %>% sample_n(5000), aes(carat, price)) +
  geom_point(alpha = 0.5) +
  geom_smooth(col="red", fill="gold")+
  theme_minimal() +
  facet_wrap( ~cut, ncol = 3)

We found a positive relationship between two variables

Chart 3 - The graph shows amount types of cut.

ggplot(diamonds, aes(cut, fill = cut)) +
  geom_bar() +
  theme_minimal()

We found Ideal had the highest number, followed by Premium, Very Good, Good and Fair has the smallest number.

Chart 4 - Total qty in each clarity

group_clarity_count <- diamonds %>%
  group_by(clarity) %>%
  summarise(count_clarity = n())

ggplot(group_clarity_count, aes(reorder (clarity , count_clarity), count_clarity, fill = count_clarity)) +
  geom_bar(stat = "identity" ) +
  theme_bw() +
  geom_text(aes(label = count_clarity), nudge_x = 0.0, nudge_y = 700) +
  ylim(0, 15000) +
  labs(x = "Clarity", y = "Count", title = " Total qty in each clarity")+
  scale_fill_gradient(low = "cyan", high = "blue")+
  coord_flip()

We found Sl1 clarity have the highest Qty

Chart 5 - Total price by diamonds cut in each colo

group_cut_color <- diamonds %>%
  group_by(cut, color) %>%
  summarise(sum_price = sum(price), .groups = "drop")

ggplot(group_cut_color, aes(cut, sum_price, fill = color)) +
  geom_col(position = "dodge") +
  theme_bw() +
  labs(x = "Diamonds cut", y = "Total price", title = "Total price by diamonds cut in each color")

We found Diamonds Ideal color G have the highest total price.
